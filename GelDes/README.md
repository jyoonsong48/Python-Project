## <img width="80" height="118" alt="ELoaded" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/1575bf9c-ce59-48e4-b35b-31cf98175e6d" /> What is Gel Electrophoresis?
$\text{Gel electrophoresis is a method for separating, analyzing, and purifying macromolecules such as nucleic acids, proteins, or their fragments based on}$ 
$\text{charge and size. When an electric current is provided, the molecules travel through the matrix at varying speeds depending on the charge.}$ 
$\text{Separation can be done based on charge and size. During the movement through the gel, larger molecules are held up on the pores}$ 
$\text{of the gel whereas smaller molecules move fast through the pores and very few will be held up on the gel pores.}$
$\text{This ultimately results in the separation of molecules.}$

<br> $\text{Description from: Sharma, A., Vijayakumar, P. S., Prabhakar, P. K., Kumar, R. (2022). Nanotechnology Applications for Food Safety and Quality}$
$\text{Monitoring. Elsevier.}$
## <img width="80" height="119" alt="tomatoo" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/a3599893-92b7-4431-be20-996a42e8ae43" /> What is pomodoro timer?
$\text{A Pomodoro timer is a productivity tool based on a time-management method that breaks work into 25-minute focused intervals}$
$\text{(called "pomodoros") separated by 5-minute breaks. Developed in the late 1980s by Francesco Cirillo,}$ 
$\text{this technique uses short, intense, distraction-free work bursts to improve focus, combat procrastination, and prevent mental burnout.}$ 
$\text{In this tool, the pomodoro is aimed for 50-minutes focused intervals to suit the gel electrophoresis running.}$ 
$\text{50/10 pomodoro is a longer, more intense variation of the traditional 25/5 Pomodoro technique}$ 
$\text{designed for complex tasks, deep work, or study sessions requiring high concentration.}$
